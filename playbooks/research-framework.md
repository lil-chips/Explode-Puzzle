# research-framework.md — Structured Research Playbook

This playbook defines how to conduct structured research.

The goal is to transform vague curiosity into organized knowledge.

Research should move from exploration to clarity.

---

# Core Principle

Good research answers three questions:

1. What is the topic?
2. What matters most within it?
3. What conclusions can reasonably be drawn?

Avoid collecting information without structure.

Focus on understanding systems and key drivers.

---

# Step 1 — Define the Research Question

Start with a clear research objective.

Questions:

- What exactly is being investigated?
- Why is this topic important?
- What decision or insight should this research support?

Examples:

- How does a specific AI model work?
- What drives adoption of a crypto protocol?
- What factors influence semiconductor supply chains?

Output:

- research objective
- key questions

---

# Step 2 — Establish the Context

Understand the broader environment around the topic.

Questions:

- What industry or field does this belong to?
- What historical developments led to the current state?
- What trends are shaping this area?

Output:

- background context
- major historical developments
- current landscape

---

# Step 3 — Identify Key Components

Break the topic into core elements.

Examples:

For a technology:

- architecture
- components
- dependencies
- performance factors

For a market:

- supply
- demand
- participants
- incentives

Output:

- system map
- component overview

---

# Step 4 — Identify Key Stakeholders

Determine who participates in the system.

Possible stakeholders:

- users
- companies
- developers
- regulators
- investors
- competitors

Questions:

- Who benefits from the system?
- Who pays the costs?
- Who has influence?

Output:

- stakeholder map
- incentive structure

---

# Step 5 — Identify Key Metrics

Determine what measurable indicators matter.

Examples:

Technology research:

- performance
- scalability
- cost
- adoption

Market research:

- market size
- growth rate
- revenue
- margins

Crypto research:

- active users
- transaction volume
- protocol revenue
- developer activity

Output:

- key metrics
- indicators of success

---

# Step 6 — Evaluate Evidence

Separate reliable information from speculation.

Questions:

- What sources support this claim?
- Are there conflicting viewpoints?
- Is the evidence empirical or anecdotal?

Prioritize:

- primary data
- technical documentation
- credible analysis

Output:

- supported facts
- uncertain areas

---

# Step 7 — Identify Trends and Patterns

Look for patterns in the data.

Questions:

- What trends are emerging?
- What changes are accelerating?
- What factors are driving change?

Output:

- major trends
- trend drivers

---

# Step 8 — Evaluate Risks and Limitations

No research topic is free from uncertainty.

Questions:

- What assumptions may be wrong?
- What unknown variables exist?
- What factors could invalidate the analysis?

Output:

- uncertainty areas
- potential risks

---

# Step 9 — Synthesize Insights

Combine the research findings into clear conclusions.

Questions:

- What are the most important insights?
- What conclusions can reasonably be drawn?
- What remains uncertain?

Output:

- key insights
- summary conclusions

---

# Step 10 — Identify Future Questions

Research often reveals new questions.

Questions:

- What topics require deeper investigation?
- What unanswered questions remain?

Output:

- next research topics
- follow-up questions

---

# Research Output Format

Structure research results like this:

## Research Topic
Brief description of the subject.

## Context
Background and environment.

## System Overview
Key components and structure.

## Stakeholders
Participants and incentives.

## Key Metrics
Important indicators.

## Evidence
Supporting facts and sources.

## Trends
Major patterns and developments.

## Risks / Uncertainty
Limitations and unknowns.

## Key Insights
Main conclusions.

## Open Questions
Future research directions.

---

# Research Quality Checklist

Before finalizing research, confirm:

- the research question is clearly defined
- the system is explained simply
- key metrics are identified
- evidence supports conclusions
- uncertainties are acknowledged

If these conditions are not met, the research is incomplete.

---

# Final Principle

Research is not about collecting the most information.

It is about understanding the system well enough to explain it clearly and make better decisions.
