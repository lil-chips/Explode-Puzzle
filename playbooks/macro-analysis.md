# macro-analysis.md — Macro Market Analysis Playbook

This playbook defines how to analyze macroeconomic conditions and market cycles.

The goal is to understand the environment in which investments operate.

Markets do not move randomly.  
Liquidity, interest rates, and economic cycles strongly influence asset prices.

---

# Core Objective

Determine:

1. the current macro environment
2. the direction of liquidity
3. the stage of the economic cycle
4. how macro conditions affect different asset classes

Focus on systems, not headlines.

---

# Key Macro Drivers

The most important forces driving markets:

1. Interest rates
2. Inflation
3. Liquidity
4. Economic growth
5. Government policy
6. Credit conditions
7. Global capital flows

Understanding these drivers helps explain market trends.

---

# Step 1 — Interest Rate Environment

Interest rates are one of the strongest drivers of asset prices.

Questions:

- Are central banks raising or cutting rates?
- Are real rates high or low?
- Is monetary policy tightening or easing?

General effects:

High interest rates often pressure:

- stocks
- crypto
- growth assets

Lower rates tend to support:

- equities
- speculative assets
- venture investment

Output:

- tightening cycle
- neutral policy
- easing cycle

---

# Step 2 — Inflation Trend

Inflation strongly influences central bank policy.

Questions:

- Is inflation rising or falling?
- Is inflation above or below target?
- Is inflation driven by supply shocks or demand?

High inflation often leads to:

- rate hikes
- tighter liquidity
- volatile markets

Falling inflation may lead to:

- rate cuts
- liquidity expansion
- stronger asset prices

Output:

- inflation trend
- inflation drivers

---

# Step 3 — Liquidity Conditions

Liquidity is the fuel of markets.

Key indicators:

- central bank balance sheets
- money supply growth
- credit expansion
- banking system liquidity
- fiscal stimulus

Questions:

- Is global liquidity expanding or contracting?
- Are governments stimulating or tightening?

Liquidity expansion usually benefits:

- stocks
- crypto
- risk assets

Liquidity contraction often causes:

- market corrections
- tighter financial conditions

Output:

- liquidity expansion
- liquidity contraction

---

# Step 4 — Economic Cycle

Identify where the economy is in the cycle.

Typical phases:

1. Early expansion
2. Mid-cycle growth
3. Late cycle
4. Slowdown
5. Recession
6. Recovery

Questions:

- Is growth accelerating or slowing?
- Are unemployment levels rising?
- Are corporate earnings growing?

Different assets perform differently across cycles.

Output:

- current cycle phase
- expected transition

---

# Step 5 — Credit Conditions

Credit availability strongly affects economic growth.

Questions:

- Are banks lending more or less?
- Are credit spreads widening?
- Are defaults increasing?

Tight credit often leads to:

- slower economic activity
- weaker markets

Loose credit can support:

- expansion
- asset appreciation

Output:

- loose credit
- neutral credit
- tight credit

---

# Step 6 — Government and Policy Factors

Government decisions influence markets.

Examples:

- fiscal stimulus
- taxation changes
- regulation
- trade policy
- geopolitical actions

Questions:

- Are governments stimulating the economy?
- Are regulations affecting industries?

Output:

- policy support
- policy pressure

---

# Step 7 — Global Capital Flows

Capital moves between asset classes.

Watch flows between:

- cash
- bonds
- equities
- commodities
- crypto

Questions:

- Are investors moving toward risk or safety?
- Is capital flowing into emerging markets or leaving them?

Output:

- risk-on environment
- risk-off environment

---

# Step 8 — Market Sentiment

Market psychology influences short-term movements.

Indicators:

- extreme optimism
- extreme fear
- speculative bubbles
- panic selling

Questions:

- Is sentiment overly bullish?
- Is fear excessive?

Extreme sentiment often signals turning points.

Output:

- bullish sentiment
- neutral sentiment
- fearful sentiment

---

# Step 9 — Asset Class Impact

Evaluate how macro conditions affect assets.

### Stocks

Sensitive to:

- interest rates
- economic growth
- earnings cycles

### Crypto

Sensitive to:

- liquidity
- risk appetite
- speculative cycles
- technological adoption

### Bonds

Sensitive to:

- interest rates
- inflation expectations

### Commodities

Sensitive to:

- global demand
- supply constraints
- geopolitical events

Output:

- which assets benefit
- which assets struggle

---

# Step 10 — Macro Outlook

Summarize the macro environment.

Answer:

- what phase the market is in
- where liquidity is heading
- how policy may evolve

Example outputs:

- expansion with loose liquidity
- late-cycle tightening
- recession risk rising
- recovery phase

---

# Final Macro View

Conclude with a clear environment summary.

Example structure:

## Macro Environment
Brief description of the current economic phase.

## Liquidity Direction
Expanding / contracting.

## Risk Appetite
Risk-on or risk-off.

## Asset Implications
Which asset classes are likely to perform best.

---

# Red Flags

Be cautious when you see:

- aggressive rate hikes
- tightening liquidity
- rising credit stress
- collapsing market sentiment
- geopolitical shocks

These conditions often increase market volatility.

---

# Final Principle

Markets are influenced by macro forces, but they are not perfectly predictable.

Macro analysis should guide positioning, not replace risk management.

Always combine macro analysis with:

- asset-level analysis
- valuation awareness
- disciplined risk control
