===== Page 7 =====
Victoria Farms scenario:
General description
“Victoria Farms” is an innovative technologically
driven Food Service distributor who uses the strategy
of franchising to make a significant difference in
distributing Fruit, Vegetables and Dairy into the
Hospitality Industry. “Franchisees” are drivers who
pick up the fresh produce from the Victoria Farms
warehouse, pack the produce, and deliver it to the
restaurants that are customers of Victoria Farms.
They work 7 days a week.
Victoria Farms’ technology has been specially
developed for their business and is largely a web
ordering, procurement, and distribution system.
There has been interest from parties in similar
businesses and industries who would like to adopt
the technology and so Victoria Farms would like to
begin to make the software a commercial product, by
initially doing a full rewrite of the existing interfaces.
===== Page 8 =====
Victoria Farms scenario:
Required functionality
The major system functions required are:
▪ Victoria Farm’s user maintenance system for
maintaining records of all the system’s users
▪  Inventory system for all available fresh
produce.
▪  Maintenance of all franchisee and customer
records, these include
• Types of orders
• Dates of orders
• Details of order deliveries
• Details of product returns
• Cost of products purchased
• Payment of products purchased
• Monthly financial reports

===== Page 16 =====
Completeness of the Product Backlog (PB)
16
• It's not about the number of PBIs in PB
• It’s about coverage of all the requirements
In the case of assignment project:
• You have to cover all requests listed in the notes made during the initial
requirements elicitation interviews with the stakeholders.
• It might be complicated to cover all this information in less than
20 PBIs…
===== Page 17 =====
Completeness of the Product Backlog,
for example…
17
• 30 PBIs, all requirements covered, no out of scope requirements
• 60 PBIs, all requirements covered, no out of scope requirements
• 30 PBIs, all requirements covered, some requirements are out of scope
• 30 PBIs, not all requirements covered, no out of scope requirements
• 60 PBIs, all requirements covered, some requirements are out of scope
• 60 PBIs, not all requirements covered, no out of scope requirements
• 30 PBIs, not all requirements covered, some requirements are out of scope
• 60 PBIs, not all requirements covered, some requirements are out of scope
Product backlog is complete ==
it covers the expected scope of the project
