V&S: 1.1 BackgroundAdapted from [K. Wiegers& J. Beatty. Software Requirements]1.Business Requirements1)Background2)Business Opportunity3)Business Objectives and Success Criteria 4)Business Assumptions and Dependencies 5)Business Risks
Employees at the company spend an average of 65 minutes per day going to cafeteria to select, purchase, and eat lunch. About 20 minutes of this time is spent walking to and from cafeteria, selecting their meals, and paying by cash or credit card. When employees go for lunch to local restaurants, they spend an average of 90 minutes off-site. Some employees phone the cafeteria in advance to order a meal to be ready for them to pick up. Employees don’t always get the selections they want because cafeteria runs out of certain items. The cafeteria wastes a significant quantity of food that is not purchased and must be thrown away.
Example: Background for Lunch Ordering System (LOS)

V&S: 1.2 Business Opportunity
Adapted from [K. Wiegers& J. Beatty. Software Requirements]
Many employees have requested a system that would permit a cafeteria user to order meals (defined as a set of one or more food items selected from the cafeteria menu) online, to be picked up at the cafeteria or delivered to the company location at a specified time and date. The LOS would save employees time, and it would increase their chance of getting the items they prefer. Knowing what food items customer want in advance, would reduce waste in the cafeteria and would improve the efficiency of cafeteria staff. The future ability for employees to order meals for delivery from local restaurants would make a wide range of choices available to employees and provide the possibility of cost savings through volume discount agreements with the restaurants.

V&S: 1.3 Business Objectives and Success Criteria
Adapted from [K. Wiegers& J. Beatty. Software Requirements]
Business ObjectivesBO-1: Reduce the cost of cafeteria food wastage by 40% within 6 months following initial release. The scale of measure to quantify the statementwill be the cost of food thrown away each week by cafeteria staff, identified via examination of Cafeteria Inventory System logs. BO-2: Reduce cafeteria operating costs by 15% within 12 months following initial release.BO-3: Increase average effective work time by 15 minutes per cafeteria-using employee per day within 6 months following initial release. Success Criteria / MetricsSM-1: 75% of employees who used the cafeteria at least 3 times per week during Q3 2013 use the new system at least once a week within 6 months following initial release.SM-2: The average rating on the quarterly cafeteria satisfaction survey increases by 0.5 on scale of 1 to 5 from Q3 2013 rating within 3 months following initial release and by 1.0 within 12 months.

V&S: 2.1 Vision Statement
Adapted from [K. Wiegers& J. Beatty. Software Requirements]
Foremployees whowant to order lunches from the cafeteria or from local restaurants online, theLunch Ordering System (LOS) isa web-based and smartphone-enabled application that will accept individual or group meal orders, process payments, and trigger delivery of the prepared meals to a designated location on campus. Unlikethe current phone and manual ordering processes, employees who use LOS will not have to go to the cafeteria to get their meals, which will save them time and will increase the food choices available to them.
