Information Radiator
An information radiator is a large and highly
visible display of critical team information
▪ typically located in a spot where the team
and others can see it constantly,
▪ is continuously updated either during the
daily stand-up meetings or even more
frequently in real-time as work is completed.

===== Page 27 =====
How to know that your velocity is good
enough to complete the project on time?
Analyze the Product backlog wrt. the duration of the project:
1. Focus on PBIs’ priorities, efforts, and the expected number of sprints
2. Don’t forget that
▪ Critical or High priority == very important, must be completed as
soon as possible
▪ Medium priority == important, should be completed within the
project, but it’s ok to complete it towards the end
▪ Low priority == “nice to have”, the project can be seen as
completed successfully even if you haven’t implemented this
functionality

===== Page 35 =====
What is the difference between
Scrum and Kanban?
Scrum:
• has sprints!
• estimates complexity of Product Backlog
Items (PBIs) in story points – this is used to
check whether the team is on track ☺
• PBIs are specified as user stories.
35
Kanban:
• has no sprints – this methodology is
based on the idea of “continuous flow”
• doesn’t require estimates for
complexity of PBIs (but doesn’t forbid
you having this addition to it)
• no rules on specifying PBIs as user
stories (it’s ok to specify items like
“login functionality”)

For this purpose Kanban boards are used

Jira Scrum:
What is different to strict Scrum?
▪ Both PBIs (user stories) and sprint tasks are called in Jira “issues”
▪ General Agile approach instead of strict Scrum Approach:
• Jira doesn’t force you to specify PBI as a user story, you need to care about this yourself
(same as with Trello)
• Sprints might be 1, 2, or 4 weeks long
• Jira product backlog isn’t a part of the board, but each sprint has a
separate board
• Jira Burn-down chart shows actual and estimated amount of work  to be
done in a sprint
• You can generate a sprint report: Burn-down chart + lists of work
completed, work not completed, and work added after sprint started
